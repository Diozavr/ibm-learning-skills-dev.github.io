Node.js Topics

Node.js Community Member - Level I, II, III

To become a Node.js Community Member, you must have an established presence in the Node.js community and be committed to learning the technology, taking the initiative to grow professionally, and support your local technology community. Holding this badge demonstrates that you understand the importance of continuous learning, and of independently exploring learning opportunities.

This badge signifies your involvement in the Node.js community. There are multiple levels of this badge, which are awarded based upon your level of participation in Node.js meetups or conferences over a period of time:

  • Level I: Awarded for participating in 3 Node.js events in 12 months.
  • Level II: Awarded for participating in 6 Node.js events in 12 months.
  • Level III: Awarded for participating in 12 Node.js events in 12 months.

How to earn the badge

 
  1. Attend and participate in the required number of Node.js meetups or conferences within a period of 12 months.
  2. Send an email to camedct@us.ibm.com with the following information:
    • Full name of the badge you are requesting
    • First and Last name
    • Email address
    • Details of badge completion, documenting each Node.js meeting or conference you attended by including a link to the event and the event dates.

 

Node.js Community Leader - Levels I, II, III, IV

To become a Node.js Community Leader, you must demonstrate active knowledge sharing in the Node.js community, which is essential for the community to grow. Holding this badge demonstrates that you have the initiative and skill to create and deliver technical content, and that you have an applied and practical understanding of the technology. Skilled presenters can quickly become a thought leader in the Node.js space.

This badge signifies your knowledge-sharing contributions to the Node.js community. There are multiple levels of this badge, awarded based upon your level of contributions accumulated through Node.js user groups, conferences, and postings (blogs, articles, podcast, video, and so on):

  • Level I: Awarded for delivering your first talk or post.
  • Level II: Awarded for delivering 5 talks or posts.
  • Level III: Awarded for delivering 15 talks or posts.
  • Level IV: Awarded for delivering 30 talks or posts.
 

How to earn the badge

  1. Deliver the required number verifiable contributions to the Node.js community by presenting Node.js talks (to a user group or conference), or posting original content (through blogs, published articles, videos, or podcasts) to demonstrate your commitment to knowledge sharing. Any combination of talks and posts is permitted.
  2. Send an email to camedct@us.ibm.com with the following information:
    • Full name of the badge you are requesting
    • First and Last name
    • Email address
    • Details of badge completion, documenting each Node.js meeting or conference talks you did or postings you made to show your commitment to knowledge sharing. Provide links as possible. All links will be reviewed by a SME for applicability.

 

Node.js Contributor

The Node.js Contributor badge signifies a dedication to Node.js technology, as well as an understanding of the Node project, its goals, and the needs of the developer community.

This badge holder is committed to the success of Node.js through direct contributions to the core Node framework, or to one of the top Node modules on the npm website.

To earn this badge, you must contribute to the core Node project itself, or contribute to one of the top downloaded packages on the npm website.

 

How to earn the badge

  1. Either:
    • Make a verifiable contribution to the nodejs/node Github repository (using your Github ID), or
    • Contribute to one of these npm most-starred modules or these most-depended-upon modules.
  2. Send an email to camedct@us.ibm.com with the following information:
    • Full name of the badge you are requesting
    • First and Last name
    • Email address
    • Details of badge completion, documenting your verifiable contribution. Include your Github username, along with the name and link to either the merged commit (for a node core contribution) or npm module.

 

LoopBack Topics

LoopBack Advocate

To become a LoopBack Advocate, you must demonstrate active knowledge sharing in the LoopBack and Node communities. Holding this badge demonstrates that you have the initiative and skill to create and deliver technical content, and that you have an applied and practical understanding of the technology. Skilled presenters can quickly become a thought leader in the LoopBack and Node space.

This badge signifies your knowledge-sharing contributions to the LoopBack community. There are multiple levels of this badge, awarded based upon your level of contributions accumulated through LoopBack user groups, conferences, and postings (blogs, articles, podcast, video, and so on):

  • Level I: Awarded for delivering your first presentation or post.
  • Level II: Awarded for delivering 5 presentations or posts.
  • Level III: Awarded for delivering 10 presentations or posts.
 

How to earn the badge

  1. Deliver the required number verifiable contributions to the LoopBack community by presenting a user group or conference session, or posting original content (through blogs, published articles, videos, or podcasts) to demonstrate your commitment to knowledge sharing on LoopBack and/or Node.js topics. Any combination of talks and posts is permitted.
  2. Send an email to camedct@us.ibm.com with the following information:
    • Full name of the badge you are requesting
    • First and Last name
    • Email address
    • Details of badge completion, documenting each contribution by including a link to the talk (on meetup.com or conference website) or post. All posts will be reviewed by a SME for applicability.

 

LoopBack Developer

 

The LoopBack Developer badge signifies a dedication to Node.js and LoopBack technology and an advanced understanding of the LoopBack framework.

To earn the badge, you must demonstrate a commitment to the success of Node.js and LoopBack through direct contributions to the LoopBack framework (in the form of accepted Pull Request), or by creating related LoopBack plugins, mixins, or components that help progress the framework.

There are multiple levels of this badge, awarded based upon your contributions to the LoopBack framework:

  • Level I: Awarded for contributing 1 accepted Pull Request or plugin.
  • Level II: Awarded for contributing 5 accepted Pull Requests or plugins.
  • Level III: Awarded for contributing 10 accepted Pull Requests or plugins.
 

How to earn the badge

  1. Deliver the required number of either:
    • LoopBack Pull Request (must be accepted by the LoopBack review board), or
    • External plugins, mixins, or components for LoopBack.
  2. Send an email to camedct@us.ibm.com with the following information:
  • Full name of the badge you are requesting
  • First and Last name
  • Email address
  • Details of badge completion, documenting your verifiable contribution. Include a link to either the pull request or the external component project, as appropriate.

How do I earn an IBM Cloud badge?

In most cases, you will be required to complete a learning activity along with an end-of-course assessment. In some cases, you may be required to complete multiple learning activities and assessments, or submit evidence of your qualification depending upon the badged activity.

 

How do I get my badge?

After you have satisfied the necessary criteria, you will receive an email from Credly's Acclaim service ( admin@youracclaim.com ) within three to five business days, which will have instructions for accepting your badge. Click the Accept button in the email to claim your badge. You will be re-directed to Credly's Acclaim, the IBM badge program administration partner. If it is your first time earning an IBM badge, you will be asked to create a profile. You can then add the badge to your profile.

 

Credly's Acclaim is a third party badge administration partner for IBM that is widely accepted throughout the industry for issuing and managing verified and trusted digital badges. Credly's Acclaim uses your name and email address details (which are provided by IBM) to facilitate the badge issuing transaction process.

 

If you do not wish to claim your badge, simply ignore the email you receive from Credly's Acclaim service.
